Know before you go

FAQ & Rules

How does it all work?

Enter the festival on Friday, or the morning of Saturday, and set up your campsite. Kayak and canoe rentals run from Bone's Kayak & Campground. You will receive a boat rental link once you purchase your River Dance ticket. The shuttle to the drop-in spot runs Saturday from 9am to 12pm — once you're on the water, the float back to the festival takes two to four hours. Miss the shuttle, miss the river, so don't sleep in.

What should I bring?

Tent, sleeping setup, river shoes, sun protection, a costume or three, and a refillable water bottle.

What's the deal with food and water?

Your ticket includes dinner on Saturday evening. A food vendor will be on site Friday with food available for purchase. Potable water is available on the grounds. Please bring your own reusable water bottle. Pack some snacks to keep you going between meals and out on the river. (Gluten-free, vegetarian, and vegan food options available both Friday and Saturday.)

Can I bring my own drinks? Glass?

Plastic and metal water containers are permitted. No glass is permitted at River Dance, period.

Is River Dance 21+?

Yes. River Dance is a 21 and up event.

Can I swim in the river?

You can swim during the float. Once you're back ashore, the swimming is done — no getting back in the water after the float.

Where can I go on the grounds?

Stay within the clearly marked, fenced-in festival area at all times. Anything outside the fence is off-limits. If we catch you out of bounds, we will call your boss and tell them what you're really doing.

Can I bring my pet?

No pets allowed. Please leave your furry friends at home.

What about weather, cell service, and parking?

It's a river valley in Tennessee in August. Plan for heat, pop-up rain, and spotty service. Parking is on site. River Dance is rain or shine. Should we need to cancel for extreme weather, tickets will be refunded at point of purchase.
